# Part XX — The Circular Convolution of Prime Constellations in Congruence Phase Space

*Volume II of the Arithmetic Geodynamics programme on the 6N skeleton.*

Volume I established the vertical law `d ln ρ_m / d ℓ → −m` (proper depth
ℓ = ln ln X) and, in its Review, the horizontal (inter-centre) correlation as a
quasi-periodic congruence diffraction pattern. **This paper recasts that
horizontal correlation in continuous form.**

The `dead(q)` mechanism becomes a **castellated indicator function** on the
phase-space torus `T¹`: for a prime q > 3 the twin's two forbidden residues cut
two holes of width 1/q. The joint survival of twins at centres N and N+j is the
**circular-convolution overlap** of the indicator with a copy shifted by j/q
(strictly an autocorrelation — equal to the convolution because the twin's hole
pattern is even). For integer lag j this integral is **exactly** the discrete
count of co-surviving residues, so the picture is a rigorous reformulation of the
singular-series ratio, not an approximation.

### The three interference states (Twin–Twin)

| state | condition | blocked | R_q(j) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A** constructive | j ≡ 0 (mod q) | 2/q | q/(q−2) > 1 |
| **B** partial | j ≡ ±3⁻¹ (mod q) | 3/q | q(q−3)/(q−2)² > 1 |
| **C** destructive | otherwise | 4/q | q(q−4)/(q−2)² < 1 |

**The quadruplet limit.** At j = 1 (adjacent twins merging into the quadruplet
6N−1, 6N+1, 6N+5, 6N+7) every prime is in State C, so

> R(1) = ∏_q R_q(1) = 𝔖_quad / 𝔖_twin² ≈ **0.3969**,   with R₅(1) = 5/9.

This is the suppression of the quadruplet **prefactor** (its X-independent
singular series). It is **orthogonal** to the −m proper-depth slope of Volume I:
`ln ρ_m = ln 𝔖_m − m·ℓ` splits into an intercept (where interference lives) and a
slope (the bare member count). **Interference sets the constant; dimension sets
the decay rate.** Conflating them would re-merge the two axes the framework
separates.

### Spectral cutoff k\*(ε)

Per-prime deviation is O(1/q) for States A, B and O(1/q²) for State C; for fixed j
all primes q > 3j+1 are State C, so the product converges and truncates at k\*(ε):

| ε | k\* | primes retained |
|---|---|---|
| 10⁻¹ | 13 | 4 |
| 10⁻² | 73 | 19 |
| 10⁻³ | 557 | 100 |
| 10⁻⁴ | 4229 | 577 |
